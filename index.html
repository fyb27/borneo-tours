Things to do @ Borneo Outback Tours

Embark on jungle trekking through lush trails, navigate pristine rivers, discover unique wildlife, and immerse in local cultures. Adventure awaits!

PUNGITON CAVE

Well known as a sacred cave by the Villagers. It is nestled approximately 15minutes by river from Labang Village and is consisted of multiple levels. Visitors will be able to marvel at the different faunas as well as rock formations in this Legendary Cave. This would be a guided trek into the cave and the average time to complete the trek would take approximately 2 hours.

Learn more

BATU PUNGGUL PINNACLE

Surrounded by untouched primary forests, trekking through the virgin jungles is a bliss. Trekking to the foot of Batu Punggul from the River Banks would take approximately 45 minutes. Upon reaching the foot of Batu Punggul, guests will have a chance to free scale up this 300 meters Limestone Outcrop! A must do for all visiting the Sapulot Area.

Learn more

LONGBOAT RIDE TO KALIMANTAN BORDER

This activity is best suited for Adrenalin Seekers! Depart from Salung Jetty and Rapid Shoot through exciting rapids all the way to the Kalimantan Border on a motorized wooden long boat navigated by skillful village boatman. Visitors will be able to witness the different Murut Villages and their livelihood along the rivers both downstream and upstream.

Learn more

VANGKAAKON WATERFALL POOL

Rest, Relax and Take-a-Dip in one of Mother Nature's best gift to us "A Natural Spa", surrounded by the beautiful scene and sounds of the forest. Nothing beats a relaxation by untouched waterfalls hidden from commercialization.

Learn more

ROMOL ECO-VILLAGE LONG HOUSE

After a scrumptious dinner prepared by the Murut Ladies, indulge in not only watching but participating and learning the Murut Traditional Dance and Musical Instrument, which will be passionately performed by the Murut Children.

Learn more

RICE WINE

A must try for all guests at Romol Eco Village. Prepared by the villagers in accordance to their strict customs abiding to all taboo believes being passed down through generations. Served in a Jar and sip through a Bamboo Straw and accompanied by small bites of comfort food.

Learn more

What is included?

All meals, accommodations, activities as mentioned in the package, all 4x4 transfers between activity areas, return transfers Keningau - Sapulot, guides, coffee & tea, drinking water and community fees.

What is not included?

Other personal expenses.

What to bring?

Sunscreen, insect repellent, raincoat, toiletries, cash (no ATM in this area), hat, trekking shoes, leech socks, flashlight, swimming attire, sandals or flip flop, camera, rubber shoes with cleats- locally known as “Adidas Kampung”

Recommended for?

Couples and groups who are interested in discovering the off beaten places, moderately adventurous activities such as caving, jungle trekking, climbing the Batu Punggul Pinnacle, boat ride to the Kalimantan Border using long wooden boat, the communities and their cultures.

Families who are interested in knowing sustainable food production. Everyone who are eager to know more about the environment in Sapulot - which includes the local nature and cultures.

Families who are interested in knowing sustainable food production and integrated farming practices.
