Orou Sapulot

Hidden World of North Borneo

What and where is Orou Sapulot?

Orou Sapulot is a community-based eco-tourism project deep in the Sapulot region of interior Sabah, Malaysian Borneo, close to the Kalimantan border. In the Murut language, orou means sun: the name reflects the project's mission to shine a light on the rainforest, rivers and living culture of Murut country, and to protect them from illegal logging and the slow erosion of tradition.

Founded by Dr. Richard Sakian Gunting, a Murut leader descended from the longhouse at Romol village, Orou Sapulot is owned and run by the Murut community itself. Tours are operated exclusively by Borneo Outback Tours, the community's own company, so every visit directly funds village incomes, the education fund and the rehabilitation of the Kabulongou forest. Guests travel overland from Kota Kinabalu through Keningau, finishing with a longboat journey up the Sapulot River into country few travellers ever see.

Things to do at Orou Sapulot

Orou Sapulot packs a remarkable amount of wild Borneo into one river valley. These are the experiences the community guides know best:

  • Climb Batu Punggul, the sacred limestone pinnacle rising sheer out of primary rainforest
  • Explore Pungiton Cave, a sacred system of chambers, swiftlets and underground river passages
  • Swim beneath the Kabulongou waterfalls and jungle plunge pools
  • Ride longboats up the Sapulot River between karst cliffs and untouched forest
  • Join a cultural night: the magunatip bamboo dance, the Lansaran jumping floor and tapai rice wine shared straight from the jar
  • Sleep in the Romol Eco-Village longhouse and jungle camps at Vangkaakon and Pungiton

Founded by the Murut, run by the Murut

Many tours in Borneo are run by outside operators who bring guests in and take profits out. Orou Sapulot is different: it was founded by Murut people and it is still run by Murut families today. The guides who lead your treks, the boatmen who read the river, and the families who brew the tapai and dance the magunatip are the owners of this project, not its employees. That is what community-based tourism means here: the forest is worth more standing than logged, and the culture is strongest when it is lived rather than displayed.

borneo.tours is the official website of Orou Sapulot, owned and run by the founding family and the Murut community of Sapulot.

Getting to Orou Sapulot

Orou Sapulot lies in Sabah's deep interior, and the journey is part of the experience. From Kota Kinabalu the road crosses the Crocker Range to Keningau, the market town that serves as the gateway to Murut country, then continues south through Nabawan district to Sapulot. The final stretch is by 4WD and longboat. All transport is arranged as part of your package: you are met in Kota Kinabalu and everything from there is handled by the community's own drivers and boatmen. The full Sapulot guide covers the route in detail.

When to visit

Tours run year-round. Rain is part of interior Borneo in every season, and the rivers, caves and cultural life carry on regardless; guides simply adjust the day around the weather. For a month-by-month picture of the seasons, see the Borneo weather guide and the best time to visit Sabah.

Community project effort

OROU Sapulot is actively involved in partnership programs with the villagers of Sapulot. One of the main goals of these programs is to offer the villagers opportunities for generating income. The expected direct and indirect impacts on participating villagers include enhanced livelihoods, educational opportunities, strengthened cultural preservation, and improved environmental stewardship. By engaging local residents in sustainable tourism practices, OROU aims to foster a sense of ownership and pride in their natural heritage, while also promoting a model of eco-tourism that benefits both the community and the ecosystem.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Orou Sapulot mean? In the Murut language, orou means sun. Sapulot is the region of interior Sabah where the project is based, so Orou Sapulot roughly translates as the sun of Sapulot: a name chosen to reflect the project's aim of bringing new life to the area's forest and culture.

Where is Orou Sapulot? Orou Sapulot is in the Sapulot region of interior Sabah, Malaysian Borneo, in Nabawan district near the Kalimantan border. It is reached overland from Kota Kinabalu via Keningau, with the final leg by 4WD and longboat.

How do I visit Orou Sapulot? Visits are arranged as packaged tours with Borneo Outback Tours, the community's own operator. Packages run from 2 days 1 night to 7 days 6 nights and include transport from Kota Kinabalu, guides, meals and accommodation. Book through the packages section or WhatsApp +60 19-227 7077.

How many days do you need at Orou Sapulot? Three to five days suits most visitors: enough for Batu Punggul, Pungiton Cave, river journeys and a cultural night without rushing. Shorter 2-day trips focus on one or two highlights.

Who runs Orou Sapulot? The Murut community of Sapulot. The project was founded by Dr. Richard Sakian Gunting, a Murut leader from the Romol longhouse, and guides, boatmen and hosts are all local community members. Borneo Outback Tours is the community's own tour company.

Do I need to be fit to visit? Moderate fitness covers most activities, and itineraries are tailored to each group. The Batu Punggul climb is the most demanding option and can be swapped for gentler river and cultural experiences.
